The Science

What's really happening when stress makes you hungry

Your body isn't broken — it's doing exactly what it evolved to do. Four systems work together to drive stress-eating, and understanding them is the first step to interrupting the cycle.

🧠

HPA Axis & Cortisol

When you experience stress, the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis floods your body with cortisol. This mobilises blood glucose, promotes visceral fat storage, and powerfully stimulates appetite — especially for calorie-dense foods.

HPA axisCortisol
↗ Tsigos & Chrousos, 2002 (NIH)
⚖️

Leptin & Ghrelin

Stress suppresses leptin — your satiety signal — while elevating ghrelin, the hunger hormone. Together they create intense food cravings even when you have adequate energy stores.

Leptin ↓Ghrelin ↑
↗ Taheri et al. (NIH)
🎯

Dopamine & Reward

Sweet, fatty, salty foods trigger a dopamine hit in your brain's nucleus accumbens. Under stress, this signal is amplified — your brain learns to use food as emotional medicine, deepening the habit loop.

Dopamine loopReward pathway
↗ Dallman et al. (NIH)
📈

Insulin Resistance

Repeated cortisol-driven glucose spikes reduce cellular insulin sensitivity over time. Stress-eating high-glycaemic foods compounds this risk, increasing the likelihood of metabolic syndrome — especially with elevated BMI.

GlucoseMetabolic risk
↗ Kivimäki & Steptoe (NIH)
The Cycle

How a moment of stress becomes a habit

Four steps — and each one makes the next harder to interrupt. Until you see them clearly.

1
Stressor perceived — your brain flags a threat. The HPA axis activates within seconds, and cortisol begins rising from the adrenal glands.
↓
2
Hormones shift — cortisol climbs, leptin falls, ghrelin rises. Appetite surges — even if you ate an hour ago. Your prefrontal cortex begins to lose ground to limbic impulse.
↓
3
You reach for food — a dopamine hit from something sweet or salty provides brief relief. The HPA axis quiets marginally. The stress response temporarily eases.
↓
4
The pattern hardens — your brain files this away: stress → food → relief. Each repetition deepens the groove and raises your baseline cortisol. This is exactly where StressReset steps in.
